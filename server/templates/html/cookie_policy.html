1. WHAT ARE COOKIES?
Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device (computer, smartphone, tablet) when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently, provide a better user experience, and deliver information to website owners.
Cookies can be "session cookies" (deleted when you close your browser) or "persistent cookies" (remain on your device until they expire or you delete them).
2. TYPES OF COOKIES WE USE
We use the following types of cookies on our Website:
Cookie Type Purpose Examples
Essential Cookies Necessary for the Website to function properly. These cookies enable core features like security, network management, and accessibility. You cannot opt out of these cookies. Session management, authentication, load balancing, shopping cart functionality
Performance Cookies Collect information about how visitors use the Website, such as which pages are visited most often. This helps us improve the Website's performance and user experience. Google Analytics, website traffic analysis, page load times
Functional Cookies Remember your preferences and settings to provide a personalized experience (e.g., language, region, login details). Saved preferences, remember me functionality, customized content
Marketing/Advertising Cookies Track your browsing activity to deliver targeted advertisements on our Website and third-party platforms. These cookies may be set by us or our advertising partners. Facebook Pixel, Google Ads, retargeting campaigns, social media ads
3. FIRST-PARTY VS. THIRD-PARTY COOKIES
First-Party Cookies: Set directly by {{BusinessName}} to enable Website functionality and collect analytics.
Third-Party Cookies: Set by external services we use, such as:
  • Google Analytics: Tracks website usage and traffic patterns
  • Facebook Pixel: Tracks conversions and enables targeted ads on Facebook and Instagram
  • Payment Processors: Stripe, PayPal, or other payment gateways for secure transactions
  • Social Media Platforms: Instagram, YouTube, TikTok embeds and sharing features
  • Email Marketing Tools: Mailchimp, ConvertKit, or similar services for email campaigns
These third parties may use cookies to collect information about your online activities across different websites. We do not control third-party cookies. Please review their privacy policies for more information.

5. HOW TO MANAGE COOKIES
You have several options to manage or disable cookies:
A. Browser Settings
Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings. You can:
  • Block all cookies
  • Accept only first-party cookies
  • Delete cookies after each browsing session
  • Receive a notification before a cookie is set
To manage cookies, visit your browser's help section:
  • Google Chrome: Settings > Privacy and Security > Cookies and other site data
  • Safari: Preferences > Privacy > Manage Website Data
  • Firefox: Options > Privacy & Security > Cookies and Site Data
  • Microsoft Edge: Settings > Cookies and site permissions
B. Opt-Out Tools
You can opt out of targeted advertising cookies through these tools:
  • Network Advertising Initiative (NAI): optout.networkadvertising.org
  • Digital Advertising Alliance (DAA): optout.aboutads.info
  • Google Ads Settings: adssettings.google.com
  • Facebook Ad Preferences: facebook.com/adpreferences
C. Do Not Track (DNT)
Some browsers offer a "Do Not Track" (DNT) setting. However, there is no universal standard for how websites should respond to DNT signals. We currently do not respond to DNT signals.

Essential cookies are necessary for the Website to operate and cannot be disabled without significantly affecting your experience.
7. OTHER TRACKING TECHNOLOGIES
In addition to cookies, we may use other tracking technologies, including:
